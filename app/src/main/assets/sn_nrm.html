1. Natural Resource Management

Four water bodies have been renovated by NICRA based on the water holding capacity and the land covered for cultivation.

  • Senkuttai with the area of 78,750 sq.ft and 6,571 cu.m water holding capacity enabled to recharge 18 open wells and 11 bore wells that provides supplemental irrigation nearly to 13.6 ha in that village
  • The area of Aayiramkuttai increased from 41,904 sq.ft to 56,960 sq.ft with water holding capacity of 22,999 cu.m, enabled to recharge 45 open wells and 112 bore wells and supplemental irrigation to 73.6 ha for crops cultivation.
  • Periyakalingukuttai with an area increased from 12,843 sq.ft. to 54,064 sq.ft with water holding capacity of 16, 468 cu.m. It enhanced to recharge 45 open wells and 156 bore wells which resulted in increased area of cultivation of 137.5 ha.
  • Ponnankannikuttai with an area of 17,265 sq.ft was repaired and renovated to an area of 48,048 sq.ft with a water holding capacity of 12,453 cu.m. It assisted to recharge 27 open wells and 75 bore wells, as a result of 29.6 ha came under cultivation.

The efforts to preserve the available water in the village by the scheme and its impact created many water storage structures in the village through convergence mode by NABARD, DRDA and departments is the success of the Natural Resource Management module of NICRA in Vadavathur and Jambumadai. By Renovation of 9 community ponds, Construction of 2 percolation ponds, 19 check dams and 27 farm ponds 68975 cu.m rain water stored in the structures during monsoon season and 235ha land brought under Rabi cultivation.

Awareness created to utilize the stored water in a more efficient manner by micro irrigation system to minimize the use of water and increase the area of cultivation for all crops and 72 ha covered till 2020.

Promotion of the insitu moisture conservation practices like summer ploughing and compartmental bunding in 300 ha gave 20% higher grain yield recorded in compartmental bunded fields. Crops did not show any withered condition even in 25 days dry spell.

Through MSDA scheme of the department of agriculture the practices like summer ploughing and compartmental bunding are followed by more than 50 % of the farmers in that village.

Soil Fertility Improvement

  • To sustain the soil fertility as well as to improve the crop yield, 564 soil health cards were issued along with fertilizer recommendation and covered 502 ha
  • Multigrain green manuring was demonstrated with 170 farmers in 67 ha area and got yield of small onion 10 tonnes / ac with 11% increase yield over check
  • By continuous adoption of this practice, the organic carbon level increased from 0.05% to 0.1% in 3 years and helps to retain the moisture during the dry spell
  • By seeing the results, 100 farmers adopt this practice in and around NICRA villages and spread over in 159 ha in the district
  • Now the village was adopted by the Department of Agriculture under Mission on Soil Halth Card Scheme for 100 % soil test based fertilizer application
