C. Green Fodder Cultivation

  • Green fodder supplimentation will improve the average daily milk production / average daily weight gain in livestock
  • Drought tolerant perennial grain type fodder COFS - 29 & 31 may be cultivated and fed to the animals
  • Yield:
    • Rainfed: 400–500 q/ha
    • Irrigated: 1000–1200 q/ha
  • Quantity to be fed:
    • Small Ruminents: 5 to 6 kg/day/animal
    • Cattle: 15 to 20 kg/day/animal
    • Hydrophonic fodder production may also be tried
  • Tree fodders like subabul (Leucaena leucocephala), kalyana murungai (Erythrina indica), koduka puli (Pithecellobium dulce), Glyricidia (Gliricidia sepium), and Moringa (Moringa oleifera) can be grown and fed to the animals
  • Preservation of green fodder can be done through silage making
