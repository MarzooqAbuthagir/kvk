Drought Mitigation

Erratic rainfall patterns can mean both an increase and decrease of precipitation amounts. If a farmer is not prepared for the undulation between extreme heat, drought, and extreme episodic rainfall events, then it can spell disaster through crop failure, topsoil loss, and infrastructure damage. The average rainfall of Namakkal district between 2011-2020 is 609.09 mm and till 2015 the rainfall received during North East Monsoon was high (377.40 mm). There was a severe drought in 2016 with very low rainfall (337.26 mm) and after that the rainfall received during South West Monsoon (2016-2020) is high (370.78 mm) compared to North East Monsoon (319.21 mm). It becomes unpredictable to assess the seasonal rainfall pattern in Namakkal district.

Hence it is essential to preserve the available rainwater and maximize its utilization for cropping by adopting the following methods
