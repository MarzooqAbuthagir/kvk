B. In Situ Moisture Conservation

Off-season land treatments such as summer ploughing, compartmental bunding, cultivation across the slope, conservation furrow, mulching with green manure crops, ridges & furrows, broad bed & furrows and micro catchments are the practices assist to increase the moisture status of the fields.

I. Summer Ploughing

  • Summer ploughing is the operation normally carried out during the summer months to break the hard-crusted upper layer of the soil. Deep ploughing improves the soil aeration, increases the infiltration capacity, permeability of the soil and kills the weeds, insect & disease causing organisms.
  • Cultivation across the slope helps to retain 10% more rainwater.

II. Compartmental Bunding

  • Small compartments act as a dam and store the rainfall received in the compartments for longer period
  • It is suitable for lesser rainfall areas for effective moisture conservation
  • It increases the water holding capacity of the soil and retains about 14% additional soil moisture and thereby achieves 20% higher grain yield
  • Crops did not show any moisture stress even in 25 days dry spells

III. Green Manuring - Multigrain Incorporation

  • Conservation of soil moisture by addition of organic matter by incorporation of multigrain may be done
  • Multigrain green manuring contains mixture of seeds as follows

Cereals

Maize, Cumbu, Sorghum and Minor Millets
Pulses

Green Gram, Black Gram, Horse Gram, Red Gram, Cowpea and Moth Bean
Oil Seeds

Groundnut, Sesame, Castor and Sunflower
Spices

Coriander, Cumin and Mustard
Green Manures

Sunnhemp, Daincha, Agathi, Calotropics, Neem and Kolinchi
